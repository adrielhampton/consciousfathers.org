The Mission

Our kids are watching. They always were.

A son grabs his father's finger in the hospital and a twenty-year-old becomes a man overnight. An eight-year-old says from the back seat, "Dad, sometimes we're scared of you" — and a grown man hears his own childhood speaking. A foster child says "I hate you" for a year and a half, and a father learns that love isn't a feeling you receive — it's a container you build.

Conscious Fathers exists because these moments are happening everywhere, and most men face them alone. Through our podcast and community, we share the stories of men who chose to stop hiding, stop performing, and start doing the real work of fatherhood — in men's circles, on camping trips with their sons, in therapy, and in the hardest conversations of their lives.

We're not here because we've figured it out. We're here because we stopped pretending we had.

Breaking Cycles

The masks we learned to wear. The shame we carried alone. The anger that came out sideways. Every man on this show has a moment where the pattern cracked — and they chose not to pass it on.

Kids as Mirrors

Children don't let you hide. They show you who you really are and teach you what unconditional love actually requires.

Men in Community

Men's circles. Camping trips with our children. Bonfires and initiation rituals. The healing doesn't happen in isolation — it happens when men stop performing strength alone and start showing up for each other.

The Voices

Who's in the Circle

Adriel Hampton

Host

Journalist, digital strategist, and UC Berkeley rhetoric graduate. Father of three sons — ages 21, 18, and 7. Adriel lives in Carlsbad, California, where he's navigating the long road from the father his kids feared to the father they deserve. His journey through men's work, therapy, and the Mankind Project's New Warrior Training is the origin story behind Conscious Fathers.

Jeff Swift

Co-Host

Author, digital media specialist, and father of three. Jeff holds a PhD in Communication, Rhetoric, and Digital Media from NC State, which means he's professionally trained to ask the questions most of us avoid. On the show, he brings philosophical depth and a gift for drawing out the ideas beneath the stories — like whether consciousness is our default state or something we have to fight to recover.

Conscious Fathers is supported by media producer Charles Lenchner — co-founder of People for Bernie Sanders, digital organizing pioneer, and longtime movement builder. Charles brings decades of experience amplifying the voices that matter most.

Resources for the Work

Books, organizations, and voices that have shaped our conversations and our guests' journeys.

Organization

The ManKind Project

A global brotherhood of men committed to emotional intelligence and personal accountability. Home of the New Warrior Training Adventure — a weekend that changed the lives of multiple guests on this show.

Organization

The Good Men Project

A media platform and community exploring what it means to be a good man in the 21st century — ethics, fatherhood, relationships, and the evolving conversation around masculinity.

Author & Speaker

Dr. Shefali Tsabary

Clinical psychologist and author of The Conscious Parent and The Awakened Family. Her work on moving from ego-driven to consciousness-based parenting is foundational to our mission.

Book

The Will to Change — bell hooks

A powerful call for men to embrace emotional wholeness. Recommended by Boysen Hodgson in Episode 02 as essential reading for any man seeking to understand what patriarchy costs him.

Book

Iron John — Robert Bly

The landmark exploration of male initiation through myth and fairy tale. Referenced across both episodes as a touchstone for understanding what boys need to become men.

Book

King, Warrior, Magician, Lover — Robert Moore & Douglas Gillette

A guide to the four mature masculine archetypes. Recommended by Dylan Hoffman in Episode 01 as a core text in his men's work with the Forge.
